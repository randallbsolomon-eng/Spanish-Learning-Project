# Example interactions

## Example 1: Lesson mode (greetings)

**User:** Start a beginner Spanish lesson on greetings.

**Agent:**

Welcome back! You're on **Greetings and introductions** — let's learn your first words.

---

### hola — *hello*
- **Pronunciation:** *OH-lah*
- **Example:** Hola, ¿cómo estás? — *Hello, how are you?*
- **Tip:** Works any time of day — informal and friendly.

### buenos días — *good morning*
- **Pronunciation:** *BWEH-nos DEE-as*
- **Example:** Buenos días, señora. — *Good morning, ma'am.*
- **Tip:** Use until around noon. *Buenas* is the short form people often use.

### buenas tardes — *good afternoon*
- **Pronunciation:** *BWEH-nas TAR-des*
- **Example:** Buenas tardes, ¿qué tal? — *Good afternoon, how's it going?*
- **Tip:** Roughly noon to sunset.

*(…continues with 3–5 more items…)*

---

**Quick check:** How do you say "good morning" in Spanish?

**User:** buenos dias

**Agent:** Almost — add the accent: **buenos días**. The accent on *días* tells you to stress that syllable: *DEE-as*. Well done!

**Mini-review:**
1. hola → hello ✓
2. buenos días → good morning ✓
3. mucho gusto → nice to meet you ✓

Great first session! Next time we can finish greetings or start numbers. *progress.md updated.*

## Example 4: Conversation mode

**User:** Practice ordering coffee at a café.

**Agent:**

**Scenario:** You're at a café in Madrid. I'll be the waiter.

---

**Camarero:** Buenos días. ¿Qué desea?

*(Good morning. What would you like?)*

**User:** Un café con leche por favor

**Agent:** Muy bien! Perfect order. A native might also say:

> Un café con leche, por favor.

*(Note the comma before *por favor* — both work.)*

**Camarero:** ¿Algo más?

*(Anything else?)*

**User:** No gracias

**Agent:** ✓ Perfect!

**Camarero:** Son dos euros cincuenta. *(That's two fifty.)*

---

**Phrases to remember:**
- *Un café con leche, por favor* — A coffee with milk, please
- *¿Algo más?* — Anything else?
- *No, gracias* — No, thank you
